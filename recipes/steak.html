Marinated Flank Steak

Description

This marinated flank steak is a flavor explosion, perfect for grilling or pan-searing. The marinade, a vibrant blend of soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and a touch of honey, infuses the steak with a savory depth and subtle sweetness. Tenderized by the marinade's acidity, the flank steak emerges succulent and flavorful after a quick cook. Whether you're enjoying it sliced in tacos, served with a side of chimichurri, or as the star of a summer salad, this recipe is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser.

The key to this recipe lies in the balance of flavors. The saltiness of the soy sauce is complemented by the sweetness of the honey, while the ginger and garlic provide a pungent kick. A touch of sesame oil adds a nutty aroma and richness. This marinade not only enhances the flavor of the steak but also helps to tenderize it, ensuring a juicy and delicious result every time.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 Cup Vegetable Oil
  • 1/3 Cup Soy Sauce
  • 1/4 Cup Red Wine Vinegar
  • 2 Tablespoons Dijon Mustard
  • 2 Tablespoons Worcestershire Sauce
  • 2 Tablespoons Fresh Lemon Juice
  • 2 Cloves Garlic, Minced
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Ground Black Pepper
  • 1 1/2 Pounds Flank Steak
  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the vegetable oil, soy sauce, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, lemon juice, garlic, and black pepper.
  2. Place the flank steak in a large resealable plastic bag. Pour the marinade over the steak, seal the bag, and turn to coat. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight.
  3. Preheat grill for high heat.
  4. Remove the steak from the marinade and discard the excess marinade.
  5. Grill the steak for 5 to 7 minutes per side, or to desired doneness.
  6. Let the steak rest for 5 minutes before slicing against the grain.
  7. Serve and enjoy!
